About Us

Our Vision

The Computer Studies as the lead in Information Technology Education in the region committed to empowering individuals by providing high quality education for the training and development of globally competitive IT professionals and workers in industry, business, government and the academe.

Our Mission

The Computer Studies shall strive to produce a technically competent and socially responsible individuals equipped with knowledge and skills to meet the standards for IT practitioners in the country and global community.

  1. Apply knowledge of computing fundamentals, technical concepts and practices and standards in the application of core information technology practice to the abstraction and conceptualization of solution models from defined problems and requirements.

  2. Understand best practices and standards and their applications.

  3. Identify, formulate, research literature, and analyze user needs to solve complex information systems problems, in order to reach substantiated conclusions using fundamental principles of Mathematics, computing fundamentals, technical concepts and practices in the core information systems, and relevant domain disciplines.

  4. Identify and analyze user needs and take them into account in the selection, creation, evaluation and administration of computer-based systems.

  5. Design, implement, and evaluate computer-based systems, processes, components, or programs to meet desired needs and requirements under various constraints.

  6. Integrate IT-based solutions into the user environment effectively.

  7. Create, select, adopt and apply appropriate modern techniques, resources and modern computing tools to complex computing activities, with an understanding of the limitations.

  8. Function effectively as member or leader of a development team recognizing the different roles within a team to accomplish a common goal.

  9. Assist in the creation of an effective IT project plan.

  10. Communicate effectively with the computing activities through logical writing presentations, and clear instructions.

  11. Analyze the local and global impact of computing information technology on individuals, organizations, and society.

  12. Understand professional, ethical, legal, security, religious and social issues and responsibilities in the utilization of information technology that will give meaning to life as an industry professional demonstrating the behavior following the examples of Sts. Arnold Janssen and Joseph Freinademetz.

  13. Engage in planning self-learning and improving performance as a foundation for continuing professional development.
